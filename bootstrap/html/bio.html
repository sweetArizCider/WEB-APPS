Biography

Beabadoobee bio Beabadoobee is a Filipino-born British singer-songwriter born Beatrice Kristi Ilejay Laus on June 3, 2000, in Iloilo City, Philippines. She was raised in London, England, and began her music career in 2017. Early Life and Career Beabadoobee’s music style is a blend of indie rock, space rock, alternative rock, samba, slacker rock, indie pop, and folk-pop. She has released five extended plays (EPs) under the independent label Dirty Hit, including “Lice” (2018), “Patched Up” (2018), “Loveworm” (2019), “Space Cadet” (2019), and “Our Extended Play” (2021). Music Style and Influences Beabadoobee’s music is characterized by her distinctive vocal style, introspective lyrics, and catchy melodies. She cites influences from 1990s alternative rock bands such as Alanis Morissette, The Cranberries, and The Smashing Pumpkins. Her music often explores themes of love, relationships, and self-discovery. Personal Life Beabadoobee’s stage name is derived from her Finstagram handle, which was initially used as a pseudonym. She has been open about her experiences as a young artist, including the challenges of navigating the music industry and finding her own unique sound. Awards and Recognition Beabadoobee has gained a significant following and critical acclaim for her music, with her single “Coffee” gaining over 50 million streams on Spotify. She has also collaborated with other artists, including Powfu on the remix of “Death Bed (Coffee for Your Head)”.
