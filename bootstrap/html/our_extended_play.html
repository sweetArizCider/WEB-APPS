Produced by George Daniel & Matty Healy Written by Nelson Riddle, J. Robert “Bob” Harris “Last Day On Earth” is Beabadoobee’s first single s ince the release of her debut album Fake It Flowers. Bea wrote this song with Matty Healy from The 1975. Matty also p roduced the track, alongside drummer George Daniel. In an interview for Rolling Stone, Bea said: “’Last Day on Earth’ is about all the things I would have done had I known we were going into a lockdown and t he world was going to change the way it has. It was written shortly after the first main lockdown and lyrically it’s me reflecting on how it would feel if we all knew ahead of time what was going to happen. All the things I would have done if I knew it was the last day of our old normality. I wrote and recorded the EP on a farm with Matty and George from the 1975 in the countryside. It was really nice being able to create together, my first time writing and recording in that kind of setting. I wanted to experiment on the sounds and sonics even more and the EP to me has a feeling of togetherness to it… How we’re all in this joined as one".

Produced by Pete Robertson & Joseph Rodgers Written by beabadoobee“Cologne” is the second track on Beabadoobee’s EP Our Extended Play. This song is pretty straightforward on its sexual themes. Bea explained on an interview with i-D: With “Cologne”, to be blatantly honest, I just wanted to write a song about sex and finding myself attractive.

Produced by Joseph Rodgers & Pete Robertson Written by Joseph Rodgers, Pete Robertson “Animal Noises” is the third track off Beabadoobee’s EP Our Extended Play. It reflects on the fear of growing up and emotes a feeling of nostalgia for the simple and wholesome moments of childhood. This song was produced by Matthew Healy and George Daniel from The 1975. In fact, the country feel in “Animal Noises” is quite reminiscent of the band’s “The Birthday Party” from their album Notes On A Conditional Form, which also explored this genre.

Produced by Joseph Rodgers & Pete Robertson Written by Pete Robertson, Joseph Rodgers“He Gets Me So High” is the fourth and final track on beabadoobee’s 2021 EP “Our Extended Play,” and is about having a (possibly almost addictive) crush; you always come back to them, even though you’re not really sure what about them makes you feel this way.
